How USAA Handles Roof Claims

USAA processes roof claims through a structured damage assessment system. They typically send out their own adjusters who follow strict underwriting guidelines based on the military member's policy. The initial inspection determines the scope of work, but here's the key: USAA adjusters are actually reasonable negotiators compared to other carriers. They understand that roofs aren't always textbook situations.

USAA usually issues their initial estimate within 10-14 days of claim filing. They generally follow industry standards and don't lowball as aggressively as some competitors. However, they require detailed documentation. Every deviation from their initial scope needs to be photographed, documented, and submitted with justification.

Military Contractor Tips That Actually Work

First, respect the military connection. Many USAA members are active duty or veterans. Showing up on time, maintaining professionalism, and understanding military culture goes a long way. These aren't typical homeowners—they appreciate efficiency and straight talk.

Second, submit supplemental estimates quickly. USAA's timeline expectations are tighter than other carriers. If you find additional damage, document it immediately with photos showing the relationship to the original damage area. Don't wait until you're halfway through the job.

Third, build relationships with USAA's preferred contractor network if possible. Being on their approved contractor list means faster approvals and higher supplement acceptance rates. I'm seeing contractors on this list get supplement approval within 3-5 business days versus 2-3 weeks for non-preferred contractors.

Common Mistakes That Kill Your Supplements

The biggest mistake? Submitting supplements without clear before-and-after photographic evidence. USAA requires visual proof that damage exists and relates to the covered loss. Vague descriptions get denied.

Second, contractors often don't understand USAA's "like-kind and quality" requirement. You can't upsell premium materials without specifically showing why the standard replacement won't work. Document structural issues, not just preference.

Third, missing the documentation deadline costs money. USAA typically requires supplements within 30 days of initial estimate. Submit late, and they'll deny it regardless of legitimacy.
