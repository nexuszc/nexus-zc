Progressive Roof Claims: A Contractor's Playbook

If you're handling Progressive insurance claims, you already know they're not like filing with State Farm or Allstate. Progressive operates differently—they move faster, they scrutinize estimates more carefully, and they expect documentation that holds up under review. Here's what actually works when you're dealing with their claims department.

How Progressive Handles Roof Claims

Progressive typically assigns a third-party adjuster to residential roof claims over $5,000. This is crucial because it changes your strategy. You're not negotiating with an insurance company directly; you're dealing with an independent adjuster who has financial incentive to keep claims lean. They use computer-generated estimates (Xactimate is standard) and compare them against your submitted proposal. Progressive's approval rate for initial estimates hovers around 62-65%, which means roughly one in three contractors submits something that gets kicked back for supplements.

The adjuster usually inspects within 48-72 hours of claim filing. They photograph everything, measure square footage with tools like drone imagery or physical measurement, and document the damage pattern meticulously. Your estimate needs to match their findings or exceed them with documented evidence.

Roof Claim Tips That Actually Work

First, photograph everything before the adjuster arrives. I mean everything—close-ups of damaged shingles, ridge deterioration, flashing issues, and the surrounding area context. Use timestamps and GPS coordinates if possible. Progressive adjusters respect detailed photographic evidence.

Second, write detailed scope of work descriptions. Don't just list "Replace roof." Instead, specify: "Remove existing architectural shingles, replace rotted decking in 12 sq ft area, install new ice and water shield, install premium architectural shingles, and replace flashing." Specific scope gets approval; vague scope gets challenged.

Third, build your estimate slightly above what you genuinely charge if possible. Progressive supplements average $800-$2,400 per claim. If your initial estimate is already at maximum, you have no room for negotiation. Price it at 110% of your normal rate initially.

Common Mistakes Contractors Make

The biggest mistake? Underestimating the supplemental documentation required. Progressive wants invoices for materials, proof of licensing, references to building codes, and photos keyed to specific line items. Contractors who submit sloppy paperwork wait 40+ days for approval instead of 7-10.

Second mistake: Not accounting for debris removal and permit costs separately. Progressive sometimes codes these differently, and if they're buried in your roofing labor line, they get questioned or reduced. Break them out.

Third: Ignoring the adjuster's estimate entirely. If they documented 22 sq of damage and you documented 25 sq, you need documentation proving the additional 3 sq. Don't guess.

Real Numbers

Average supplement amounts: $1,100. Range: $300-$8,900 depending on damage complexity. Approval rate for supplements: 78% approved at first submission if documentation is thorough. Approval time: 5-8 business days with complete documentation, 14-21 days with incomplete documentation.

Track your metrics by adjuster, too. Some Progressive adjusters approve supplements at 85% rates; others sit at 62%. Once you identify the reasonable ones, request them specifically on future claims.

Progressive claims require respect for detail and patience with process. Get those right, and you'll see consistent supplement approval and faster payment cycles than most carriers.
