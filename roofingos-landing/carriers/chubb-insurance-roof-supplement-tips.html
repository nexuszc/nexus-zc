Chubb Roof Supplement Tips: A Contractor's Guide

Working with Chubb insurance claims requires a different approach than most carriers. Chubb is known for being thorough—sometimes frustratingly so—but understanding their process will save you time and money on every project. Here's what you need to know about navigating Chubb roof supplements successfully.

How Chubb Handles Roof Claims

Chubb's adjusters typically conduct more detailed inspections than other carriers. They're looking at underlying damage, matching existing materials exactly, and questioning scope differences. Most importantly, they require strong documentation for any supplement requests. A Chubb adjuster won't approve a supplement based on a verbal conversation—everything needs to be in writing with photographic evidence and detailed explanations.

The typical Chubb claim process involves an initial inspection, an estimate, and then a waiting period before supplements are considered. They're less likely to approve supplemental claims if they feel the initial estimate was incomplete, so getting it right the first time matters tremendously.

Supplement Strategies That Actually Work

First, document everything during your initial walkthrough. Take photos showing roof condition, flashing details, decking issues, and anything that might constitute hidden damage. Chubb respects contractors who show their work.

Second, organize supplements by category: structural damage, material upgrades, and labor adjustments. Chubb responds better to well-organized, categorized requests than scattered additional items. Group similar items together with explanations for each category.

Third, include pricing from your suppliers. Chubb wants to see that your material costs are reasonable and current. If your supplement includes new pricing, include the quote or invoice to back it up.

Finally, timing matters. Submit supplements while the file is active and the adjuster is engaged, not weeks later. Chubb is more receptive to supplements submitted within 48-72 hours of discovering new damage.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Don't pad your supplements. Chubb has seen every trick, and padded estimates result in reduced approval rates on future claims. Contractors with a history of reasonable supplements get faster approvals.

Don't forget to address the "why." Chubb wants to know why damage wasn't discovered initially. Was it hidden under shingles? Did it reveal itself during tear-off? Explain the discovery process clearly.

Avoid submitting supplements without photos. Chubb requires visual evidence. A written description alone won't cut it. Make sure your photos are dated and clearly show the condition you're supplementing for.

Real Numbers: What You Should Expect

The average Chubb supplement ranges from $800 to $3,200 depending on roof size and damage complexity. Contractors report approval rates of 65-75% when supplements are well-documented and reasonable. Supplements exceeding 20% of the original estimate face more scrutiny and have lower approval rates.

Material supplements typically approve faster (70-80% approval) than labor adjustments (55-65% approval). Structural damage supplements have the highest approval rate at 80+%, since Chubb prioritizes safety and structural integrity.

Final Thoughts

Chubb isn't difficult if you respect their process. They want accurate claims, proper documentation, and honest supplements. Work within their system, and you'll build a reputation that leads to faster approvals and smoother projects. That's the real return on doing Chubb claims right.
