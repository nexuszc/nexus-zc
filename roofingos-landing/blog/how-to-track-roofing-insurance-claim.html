How to Track Roofing Insurance Claims

Track roofing insurance claims by creating a central system that logs claim dates, adjuster contacts, estimate amounts, payment schedules, and all photo documentation in one place. The best tracking systems let your entire team see claim status in real-time so nothing falls through the cracks.

Set Up Your Claim Tracking System

You need one place where every claim lives. Not scattered across texts, emails, and sticky notes.

Start with these essential fields for every claim:

  • Homeowner name and property address
  • Insurance company and claim number
  • Date of loss and claim filing date
  • Adjuster name and direct contact info
  • Initial estimate amount
  • Supplement requests and amounts
  • Payment status and check numbers
  • All photo documentation dates

Most roofing companies use either expensive platforms like JobNimbus ($619+/mo) or AccuLynx ($250+/mo), or they try to piece together free tools that weren't built for insurance work. CompanyCam ($99/mo) handles photos well but doesn't track claim details. If you want a jobnimbus alternative free option, Roofing OS gives you full claim tracking without the monthly hit.

The key is having every team member update the same system. Your project manager needs to see what the salesperson promised. Your crew lead needs to know if supplements got approved. Your admin needs to track which checks actually arrived.

Document Everything With Photos and Notes

Insurance adjusters deny claims because of missing documentation. Not because your roof estimate was wrong.

Take photos of everything:

  • Every shingle with hail damage
  • Wind damage patterns
  • Ridge cap deterioration
  • Granule loss in gutters
  • Flashing issues
  • Interior water damage if accessible

Date stamp matters. Your insurance claim tracking system needs to show exactly when you took each photo. This protects you when adjusters claim damage happened after the loss date.

Add notes immediately after every conversation. "Spoke with adjuster Mark at 2pm - agreed to $12,400 for north slope replacement, sending supplement for ridge vent tomorrow." These notes become your paper trail when payments stall.

Your roofing supplement checklist xactimate should tie directly to your claim tracking. When you request a $2,800 supplement for underlayment upgrades, log it in your tracker the same day.

Monitor Payment Milestones

Average insurance roofing claim is $8,000-$12,000. You need to track every dollar from approval to deposit.

Set up these payment checkpoints:

  • Initial approval date and amount
  • Deductible collected (always get this upfront)
  • First insurance check received
  • Recoverable depreciation amount
  • Final payment after completion
  • Supplement approvals and payment

Most insurance companies send two checks - one upfront (actual cash value minus deductible) and one after completion (recoverable depreciation). If you're not tracking both, you're leaving money on the table.

Flag claims that hit 30 days without payment. Something's wrong. Either the homeowner didn't sign the check over, the insurance company "lost" paperwork, or your completion photos never got submitted.

Share Status With Homeowners

Homeowners call you daily asking "where's my claim at?" because they're stressed about insurance.

Give them a portal where they see:

  • Current claim status
  • What you're waiting on from insurance
  • Photos you've submitted
  • Approved amounts
  • Project timeline

This cuts phone calls in half. Homeowners check the portal instead of interrupting your crew.

Frequently Asked Questions

What information should I collect from insurance adjusters?

Get their direct phone number, email, claim number, and preferred contact method. Ask when they typically respond and if they have an assistant who handles follow-ups. Document which line items they approved and which they flagged for review.

How long does the average roofing insurance claim take?

Most claims take 30-45 days from initial inspection to final payment. Simple claims with cooperative adjusters can close in 2-3 weeks. Complex claims with multiple supplements or disputes can stretch 90+ days.

Should I track insurance claims separately from my regular jobs?

No. Insurance claims are jobs that happen to have insurance funding. Track them in the same system so you see your complete production schedule. Just add insurance-specific fields like adjuster contacts and supplement status.

What happens if I lose documentation on an insurance claim?

You'll likely eat the cost of any supplements or disputed line items. Insurance companies won't pay for work you can't prove was needed. Always keep backup copies of photos, estimates, and adjuster communications in your tracking system.
