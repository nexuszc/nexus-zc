CompanyCam: What It Does and Who It's For

CompanyCam launched as a photo-first tool for contractors, and it's genuinely excellent at what it does. You can take job site photos, organize them by project, annotate images, and share them with your team or customers in a clean, professional way. Their timeline view, GPS tagging, and report generation are well-designed and reliable.

If your crew is constantly losing track of before-and-after photos, or you're tired of photos buried in someone's camera roll, CompanyCam solves that problem cleanly. It's also built for all trades — not just roofers — so if you run a mixed contracting business, that flexibility matters.

Who it's for: Contractors who already have solid job management software and just need a dedicated, polished photo documentation layer on top of it.

Pricing: Starts at $99/month for photo documentation. Additional features and team seats can push costs higher. There's no free plan.

The honest limitation: CompanyCam doesn't do estimates, insurance supplements, homeowner portals, canvassing, or claim workflows. You'll still need three or four other tools to run a roofing business alongside it.
