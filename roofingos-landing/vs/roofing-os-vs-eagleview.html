Roofing OS vs Eagleview: Which Is Better for Roofing Contractors in 2026?

Quick Answer: Eagleview wins if you need best-in-class aerial measurement reports and nothing else. Roofing OS wins if you want an all-in-one platform to run your entire roofing business — from leads and estimates to crew scheduling, insurance claims, and homeowner communication — at a fraction of the cost.

Eagleview: What It Does and Who It's For

Eagleview has been around since 2008 and built a strong reputation doing one thing exceptionally well: aerial roof measurements. Using satellite and aerial imagery, they generate detailed measurement reports — total square footage, pitches, ridges, valleys, hips — that are widely accepted by insurance carriers and used by adjusters across the country.

If you're a large commercial roofing company or an adjuster who needs a defensible, carrier-accepted measurement document, Eagleview delivers. Their reports are polished, their data is accurate, and their name carries weight in the insurance world.

The catch? Each report costs around $150. That's per job. If you're running 20 insurance jobs a month, you're spending $3,000 monthly just on measurements — before you've touched a single piece of software to manage those jobs. Eagleview is a measurement tool, not a business management platform. It doesn't help you track leads, schedule crews, follow up with homeowners, manage supplements, or run your operations. You still need other tools for all of that.

Eagleview is best suited for established contractors and adjusters who specifically need carrier-grade aerial reports and already have other systems in place to run the rest of their business.

When to Choose Eagleview

I'll be straight with you — there are real situations where Eagleview is the right call:

  • You need carrier-accepted measurement reports. If your insurance carrier or adjuster specifically requires an Eagleview report, that's the answer. Their brand recognition with carriers is real and earned.
  • You're an independent adjuster or large commercial operation that only needs measurement documentation and already has enterprise-level software handling everything else.
  • Your volume is low and per-report cost isn't a concern. If you're doing two or three insurance jobs a month and just need a solid report to hand over, $150 per report might be worth it for the credibility alone.
