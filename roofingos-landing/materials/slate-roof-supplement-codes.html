Slate Roofing Supplement Codes: A Comprehensive Guide for Contractors

Understanding Slate Roofing Specifics for Insurance Claims

Slate roofing represents one of the most valuable and complex roofing materials in the insurance restoration industry. Unlike asphalt shingles or metal roofing, slate requires specialized knowledge, expertise, and material sourcing. As a contractor, understanding that slate is classified as a premium, labor-intensive material is crucial for accurate estimation and claim documentation. Slate tiles are typically 3/16 to 1/4 inch thick, weigh 600-900 pounds per roofing square, and demand precision installation. Insurance adjusters often underestimate slate restoration costs, making thorough documentation and proper coding essential to justify your estimates and secure appropriate reimbursement.

Essential Xactimate Line Items and Codes for Slate Roofing

When creating estimates in Xactimate, utilize code "07310 Slate Roofing" as your primary category. For slate replacement, specify "07310 Slate Roofing - Remove & Replace" with careful attention to square footage. Use "07310-R" for removal of damaged slate and "07310-N" for new installation. Flashing work requires separate coding: "07600 Flashing & Sheet Metal" with subcodes for valley flashing, ridge caps, and chimney flashing specific to slate applications. Underlayment beneath slate demands code "07570 Synthetic Underlayment" or "07580 Roofing Felt," as modern slate installations require breathable synthetic materials rather than traditional felt. For ridge and hip tiles, use code "07310-RH" to itemize these specialty components separately. Always include "07120 Roof Demolition" to account for careful removal procedures that slate requires to preserve salvageable material.

Countering Common Insurance Adjuster Pushback

Insurance adjusters frequently attempt to depreciate slate roofing or suggest replacement with lower-cost materials. Counter this approach by providing historical replacement cost documentation proving slate's longevity—typically 75-200 years—which justifies premium pricing. When adjusters suggest asphalt substitution, explain that slate roofing is a unique architectural feature affecting property value and cannot be equivalently replaced. For age-related depreciation arguments, reference the National Roofing Contractors Association (NRCA) standards establishing that slate doesn't deteriorate at the same rate as conventional materials. Document any architectural significance or historical property designation that mandates slate replacement. Request the adjuster's specific code of ethics references if they deny coverage for like-kind material replacement, as most policies mandate restoration to pre-loss condition.

Proper Documentation of Slate Roofing Damage

Photograph all damage from multiple angles, clearly showing individual slate tile fractures, missing tiles, and underlying damage patterns. Document the original material's age, quality, and condition through close-up photography and written notes. Create a damage map indicating which roof sections show impact damage, water penetration evidence, and displaced flashing. For hail claims, photograph the dent diameter on slate samples alongside a measuring device. Document any existing repairs or previous damage to establish pre-loss conditions. Obtain written assessments from structural engineers if water damage has compromised underlying materials or if structural integrity is questioned.

Supplement Tracking Tips for Slate Roofing Projects

Maintain a detailed project log documenting all initial estimate discussions, adjuster communications, and material specification changes. Create a supplemental tracking spreadsheet listing each supplement with corresponding Xactimate codes, submission dates, and approval status. For slate projects, anticipate supplements due to concealed water damage, underlying structural deterioration, and specialty flashing requirements discovered during removal. Track material lead times separately, as specialty slate sourcing can extend project timelines significantly. Maintain copies of all communication with adjusters regarding scope disputes, and document any field conditions variations from the initial inspection. This comprehensive tracking protects your claims process and provides evidence for dispute resolution if supplemental payments are denied.
