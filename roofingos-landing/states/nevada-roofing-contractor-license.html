Nevada Contractor License for Roofing Contractors

Nevada Specific Requirements for Contractor License

In Nevada, roofing contractors must obtain a license from the Nevada Contractors Board before legally performing roofing work. The process begins with meeting basic eligibility requirements: you must be at least 18 years old, have a valid Social Security number or Federal Tax ID, and maintain a Nevada business address. Unlike some states, Nevada does not require a specific roofing license classification; instead, roofers typically operate under the "General Contractor" or "Specialty Contractor" categories depending on their scope of work.

The Nevada Contractors Board requires applicants to pass a written examination covering Nevada construction law, safety regulations, and general contracting knowledge. You'll need to submit proof of workers' compensation insurance, a Nevada business license, and a contractor's bond—typically $10,000 for specialty contractors or $25,000 for general contractors. Application fees currently run around $175, though this varies based on license classification. Processing times typically take 4-6 weeks, so plan accordingly before scheduling jobs.

Nevada requires contractors to renew their licenses every two years. Renewal applications must include updated proof of insurance, continued payment of bond premiums, and verification that your business address remains valid. Missing renewal deadlines can result in license suspension and significant penalties.

What Contractors Need to Know Before Starting Jobs in Nevada

Before beginning any roofing project in Nevada, verify that your license is current and active with the Contractors Board. Post your license number clearly on all marketing materials, invoices, and job sites—this isn't optional. Nevada law requires that homeowners and clients see your license information before work commences.

Nevada has strict lien rights laws. You must provide customers with a lien notice within 10 days of starting work. Failure to do so can jeopardize your ability to collect payment through the lien process. Always document your work with photographs and maintain detailed time and material records.

The state requires written contracts for all roofing projects exceeding $1,000. Contracts must include scope of work, timeline, payment terms, and warranty information. Nevada also mandates a mandatory three-day cancellation period for customers—they can cancel the contract without penalty within three days of signing. Make sure your contract clearly states this requirement.

Common Pitfalls in Nevada

Many Nevada roofers underestimate the importance of proper documentation. Operating without a current license is surprisingly common and results in hefty fines ($1,000-$5,000) and work stoppage orders. Another frequent mistake is failing to provide required disclosures before beginning work, which can expose you to legal liability.

Inadequate insurance coverage causes problems too. Nevada requires general liability coverage, and workers' compensation is mandatory if you have employees. Cutting corners here can result in license revocation.

Poor payment practices create disputes. Some contractors don't follow Nevada's specific requirements for invoicing and payment terms, leading to non-payment issues and difficulty collecting on liens.

Resources and Official Links

Visit the Nevada Contractors Board website at nv.gov/business/contractors for official licensing information, exam scheduling, and renewal procedures. The Builders License Board also maintains helpful FAQs and application forms.
