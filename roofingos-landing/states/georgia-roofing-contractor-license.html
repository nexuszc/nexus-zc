Georgia Contractor License for Roofing Contractors: A Practical Guide

Georgia-Specific Requirements for Contractor License

In Georgia, roofing contractors must obtain a license from the Georgia Construction Industry Licensing Board (GCILB) before performing any roofing work for compensation. The state requires contractors to pass the Roofing Contractor Exam, which tests knowledge of Georgia construction codes, safety regulations, and roofing practices. You'll need to provide proof of at least three years of roofing experience within the past five years, either as a roofer or roofing contractor. If you're applying through a company, you must designate a responsible managing employee (RME) who meets these qualifications and will oversee all roofing projects. The application process includes fingerprinting for a background check, and you must maintain general liability insurance with a minimum of $300,000 in coverage. License renewal happens every two years, and you'll need to complete continuing education requirements to maintain active status.

What Contractors Need to Know Before Starting Jobs in Georgia

Before you begin any roofing project in Georgia, verify that your license is current and in good standing with GCILB. Georgia law requires you to provide written contracts for all roofing jobs that clearly outline scope of work, materials, timeline, and total cost. You must also provide customers with a Notice of Cancellation rights within three days of signing. Always pull permits for roofing work through your local jurisdiction—this isn't optional and violations carry significant penalties. Georgia has specific wind resistance codes you must follow, particularly in coastal areas. Document everything with photographs and detailed project records. Keep accurate workers' compensation records if you have employees, as Georgia mandates this coverage. Before submitting invoices, understand that homeowner associations may require additional approvals, and some municipalities have stricter codes than state minimums.

Common Pitfalls in Georgia

One major mistake contractors make is working without proper permits, assuming verbal approval from homeowners is sufficient. Georgia takes unpermitted work seriously, and you can face fines and license suspension. Another common pitfall is underestimating insurance requirements—customers often request proof of coverage, and inadequate coverage can leave you liable. Many contractors fail to document their continuing education hours properly, leading to license renewal complications. Avoid making verbal agreements; Georgia requires written contracts, and disputes often arise when documentation is incomplete. Don't overlook local jurisdiction requirements—what's acceptable in Atlanta may not be in smaller municipalities. Additionally, some contractors neglect to update their license information when changing business structure or ownership, creating legal complications. Lastly, poor record-keeping during projects can cause issues if disputes arise later or if inspections require proof of workmanship.

Resources and Official Links

Visit the Georgia Construction Industry Licensing Board at www.gcilb.ga.gov for license applications, exam schedules, and renewal information. The Georgia Department of Labor website provides information about workers' compensation requirements. Contact your local building department for permit requirements specific to your area. The Georgia Roofing Contractors Association offers industry resources and networking opportunities. Review the Georgia Construction Code, which incorporates the International Building Code with state-specific amendments.
