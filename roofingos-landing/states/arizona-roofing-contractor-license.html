Arizona Contractor License for Roofing Contractors: A Practical Guide

Arizona-Specific Requirements for Contractor License

In Arizona, roofing contractors must obtain a license from the Registrar of Contractors (ROC) before performing any roofing work. The state requires contractors to hold either a General Contractor (GC) license with roofing endorsement or a Specialty Contractor license specifically for roofing. To qualify, you'll need to pass the Arizona Contractor Exam and demonstrate financial responsibility. The exam covers both the Arizona Contractor License Law and the National Building Code. You must also maintain a minimum level of experience—typically four years of paid work in roofing during the past ten years. Arizona requires contractors to be at least 18 years old and have a good moral character with no disqualifying criminal convictions. Additionally, you'll need a valid Arizona business license and must maintain contractors' liability insurance coverage of at least $300,000.

What You Need to Know Before Starting Jobs in Arizona

Before bidding on or starting any roofing project in Arizona, verify that your license is active and in good standing with the ROC. Arizona law requires you to display your license number on all business cards, advertisements, and written bids—this is non-negotiable. When providing estimates, you must include your license number, ROC registration number, and the address where you maintain your business records. Arizona requires a written contract for all work exceeding $1,000, and the contract must specify the roofing materials, labor costs, and timeline. You're also required to provide customers with a "Notice Regarding Dispute Resolution" before they sign any contract. Importantly, Arizona law prohibits contractors from requiring full payment before work begins; you must follow a specific payment schedule tied to project milestones.

Common Pitfalls Arizona Roofing Contractors Face

One of the most common mistakes is working without an active license or using an expired license. The ROC conducts regular audits, and operating unlicensed can result in substantial fines and legal action. Another frequent pitfall is failing to include required contract language and disclosures, which can void your contract and leave you unprotected. Many contractors underestimate insurance requirements or let coverage lapse, which violates Arizona law and exposes them to liability. Mishandling customer deposits is another major issue—Arizona has strict rules about how and when you can collect payments. Lastly, failing to maintain proper records and not documenting completion of work can create serious problems if disputes arise or if the ROC conducts an investigation.

Resources and Official Links

The Arizona Registrar of Contractors website (roc.az.gov) is your primary resource for licensing information, exam schedules, and continuing education requirements. The ROC provides the official exam study guide, which you should download and review thoroughly. The Arizona Contractors License Law is available on the ROC website and should be kept accessible for reference. For specific questions about roofing endorsements or specialty licenses, contact the ROC directly at 602-542-1525. The Arizona Department of Insurance also provides resources related to contractor bonding and insurance requirements.
