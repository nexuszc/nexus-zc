Pennsylvania Contractor License for Roofing Contractors

Pennsylvania-Specific Requirements for Contractor License

Pennsylvania requires roofing contractors to obtain a Home Improvement Contractor License before performing any roofing work on residential properties. This license is administered by the Pennsylvania Attorney General's office. To qualify, you'll need to demonstrate financial responsibility, maintain a surety bond (typically $25,000-$50,000), and carry proper insurance coverage. You must also pass a background check and meet experience requirements, which generally include at least two years of roofing experience in the past five years. Unlike some states, Pennsylvania doesn't require a written exam, but you do need to submit detailed application materials including proof of insurance, bonding information, and your business registration documentation.

What Contractors Need to Know Before Starting Jobs in Pennsylvania

Before taking on any residential roofing project in Pennsylvania, verify that your license is current and active. Pennsylvania contractors must provide customers with a written contract that includes specific disclosures about the Home Improvement Contractor License Law. This contract must be in writing, signed by both parties, and include a detailed scope of work, timeline, and payment schedule. You're required to provide customers with a three-day cancellation period, and you cannot collect any payment before work begins. Make sure you understand local permit requirements—many Pennsylvania municipalities require roofing permits, especially for new installations or significant structural work. Additionally, check if your project falls under any local homeowner association rules or historic district guidelines.

Insurance is non-negotiable. You need both general liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage. Keep copies of all certificates of insurance readily available, as customers frequently request them during the bidding process.

Common Pitfalls in Pennsylvania

One major mistake roofing contractors make is operating without proper licensing or allowing their license to lapse. Pennsylvania takes this seriously—unlicensed work can result in fines up to $1,000 per violation and legal action from customers. Another common issue is inadequate contract documentation. Vague contracts lead to disputes and complaints to the Attorney General's office. Always be specific about materials, labor costs, and project timelines.

Failing to obtain permits is another frequent problem. Pennsylvania municipalities have varying requirements, and it's your responsibility to know local regulations. Payment collection practices also trip up contractors—collecting large upfront payments violates Pennsylvania law and invites complaints. Never demand full payment before starting work; establish a reasonable payment schedule tied to project milestones instead. Finally, poor record-keeping causes compliance headaches. Document everything: contracts, change orders, insurance certificates, and customer communications.

Resources and Official Links

The Pennsylvania Attorney General's office maintains the official contractor licensing database at www.attorneygeneral.gov. You can verify your license status, check disciplinary records, and access the complete Home Improvement Contractor License Law regulations here. The Pennsylvania Department of Labor and Industry (www.dli.pa.gov) handles workers' compensation requirements and can clarify insurance obligations. For permit and local code questions, contact your municipality's building permit office directly—requirements vary significantly across Pennsylvania's counties and municipalities.
