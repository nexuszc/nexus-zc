Michigan Contractor License for Roofing Contractors

Michigan Specific Requirements for Contractor License

Michigan doesn't require a state-level contractor license for roofing work. However, this doesn't mean you can operate without credentials. You must register as a business with the Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA). Sole proprietors need an Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS, while LLCs and corporations require proper business registration with the Michigan Secretary of State.

Additionally, roofing contractors must carry workers' compensation insurance if they have employees. This is mandatory in Michigan and enforced by the Michigan Department of Insurance and Financial Services. Liability insurance isn't legally mandated, but it's essential for protecting your business and winning customer trust. Many homeowners and commercial clients won't hire you without it.

Some municipalities in Michigan require local permits and inspections for roofing work. Check with individual cities and townships where you operate, as requirements vary significantly across the state. Cities like Detroit, Grand Rapids, and Ann Arbor have specific building departments that oversee roofing projects.

What You Need to Know Before Starting Jobs in Michigan

Before bidding on roofing projects, understand Michigan's building code requirements. The state adopted the International Building Code (IBC) with state amendments. Roofing installations must comply with these standards, including specific wind resistance ratings and snow load calculations relevant to Michigan's climate.

Homeowner protection matters significantly in Michigan. If you're doing residential work, you should understand Michigan's Residential Builders' Construction Lien Act and the Home Improvement Finance Act. These laws protect consumers and require clear contracts, written estimates, and proper disclosure of payment terms. Failing to comply exposes you to liability and potential legal action.

The Michigan Department of Attorney General oversees home improvement contractor practices. Maintain proper documentation of all contracts, change orders, and correspondence with clients. This protects both you and your customers and demonstrates professionalism if disputes arise.

Common Pitfalls in Michigan

Many Michigan roofing contractors underestimate the importance of local permitting. Operating without required local permits can result in fines, liens against your work, or being forced to redo jobs. Always contact the building department before starting work.

Another common mistake is inadequate insurance documentation. Clients frequently request proof of insurance before work begins. Not having current certificates of insurance readily available costs you jobs. Make sure your insurance is always current and that you can provide updated certificates within 24 hours.

Seasonal challenges unique to Michigan often catch contractors off guard. Winter weather can delay projects and create liability concerns. Document weather-related delays and ensure your contracts address how weather impacts timelines and payment schedules.

Resources and Official Links

Visit the Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs website (michigan.gov/lara) for business registration requirements and resources. The Michigan Department of Attorney General (michigan.gov/ag) provides information on home improvement contractor regulations.

Contact your local city or township building department for specific permit requirements in your service area. The Michigan Construction Code Commission publishes building code information and updates.
