Illinois Contractor License for Roofing Contractors

Illinois Specific Requirements for Contractor License

Illinois requires roofing contractors to obtain a Roofing Contractor License through the Department of Financial and Professional Regulation (IDFPR). To qualify, you must have at least three years of documented roofing experience within the past ten years. This experience can be verified through previous employers, payroll records, or tax returns.

You'll need to pass the Illinois Roofing Contractor Exam, which tests your knowledge of roofing materials, installation methods, building codes, and business practices. The exam costs approximately $250 and covers both technical and practical aspects of roofing work. You must score at least 75% to pass.

Additionally, you need to maintain a current Illinois Business License and register with the Secretary of State if operating as a corporation or LLC. Workers' compensation insurance is mandatory if you have employees, and general liability insurance is highly recommended for all roofing operations.

What Contractors Need to Know Before Starting Jobs in Illinois

Before accepting any roofing projects in Illinois, verify that your license is current and active through the IDFPR database. Illinois requires contractors to include their license number on all contracts, quotes, and marketing materials. Failing to display your license number is a violation and can result in fines.

Illinois follows specific building codes for roofing installations, particularly the Illinois Energy Conservation Code. Your work must comply with current standards for ventilation, insulation, and fire ratings. Different municipalities may have additional requirements, so always check local ordinances before bidding.

You must provide written contracts for all roofing jobs that clearly outline scope of work, materials, timeline, and pricing. Illinois law requires a three-day cooling-off period for certain home improvement contracts, giving homeowners time to cancel without penalty. Make sure your contracts reflect this requirement.

Common Pitfalls in Illinois

One major mistake is operating without current licensing. Illinois enforces strict penalties for unlicensed contractors, including fines up to $10,000 and potential criminal charges. Always keep your license renewed at least 30 days before expiration to avoid lapses.

Another common issue is inadequate documentation of project details. Inspectors frequently cite contractors for incomplete permits or failure to maintain proper records. Document every job with photos, material specifications, and signed inspections.

Many contractors underestimate the importance of proper permit acquisition. Even small roofing repairs may require permits in certain Illinois jurisdictions. Working without permits can result in fines and complications when homeowners sell their properties.

Resources and Official Links

Visit the Illinois Department of Financial and Professional Regulation website at www.idfpr.com for license verification, application forms, and exam scheduling. The IDFPR also publishes the Illinois Roofing Contractor Act, which outlines all legal requirements.

Contact your local building department for municipal-specific codes and permit requirements. The Illinois Department of Labor provides information about workers' compensation requirements at www.cyberdriveillinois.com.
