Questions to Ask Your Roofing Contractor Before You Hire

A roof repair or replacement is a major home investment, and choosing the right contractor makes all the difference. Asking the right questions upfront protects your wallet, sets clear expectations, and ensures your contractor is qualified to do the job well.

What Are Your Credentials and Insurance?

Before anything else, verify that your contractor is legitimate and protected. Ask to see their business license, liability insurance, and workers' compensation coverage. A reputable contractor will provide these documents without hesitation. This protects you from liability if someone gets injured on your property during the work.

Also ask how long they've been in business and whether they're bonded. A bonded contractor has financial backing if they fail to complete the job or if materials are damaged.

Can You Provide References and Show Your Portfolio?

Experience matters enormously in roofing. Ask your contractor for at least three recent references from homeowners with similar projects—ideally completed within the last year. Call these references and ask specific questions: Did the work finish on time? Was the crew professional? How does the roof look now?

Also request to see photos of completed projects. A contractor confident in their work will happily show you before-and-after pictures. If they seem evasive about references or portfolio, that's a red flag.

What Materials Do You Recommend and Why?

Different roofing materials have different lifespans, costs, and appearances. Ask your contractor why they recommend a specific material for your home. Consider asking:

  • How long will the material last?
  • What is the warranty coverage?
  • How does it perform in our local climate?
  • What's the cost difference between options?
  • Does it meet local building codes?

Don't assume the cheapest option is best—a higher-quality material often saves money long-term through durability and fewer repairs.

How Much Will This Cost and What's Included?

Ask for a detailed, written estimate that breaks down labor, materials, permits, and any additional costs. A vague estimate like "around $5,000" isn't helpful. You need specifics.

Also ask:

  • Do you handle permits and inspection fees?
  • What happens if we uncover hidden damage during work?
  • Is debris removal included?
  • Do you offer payment plans?
  • What is your cancellation or change-order policy?

Never pay the full amount upfront. A typical payment schedule might be 30% down, 40% when work begins, and 30% upon completion.

What's Your Timeline and How Will You Keep Me Updated?

Ask when your contractor can start and how long the job will take. Weather delays are normal, but your contractor should give you a realistic timeline. Ask about their process for communicating updates—will they call, text, or email? How often should you expect to hear from them?

A professional contractor uses project management tools to keep homeowners informed every step of the way. Many modern contractors use software like Roofing OS, which sends you photos, progress updates, and timelines directly—so you're never left wondering what's happening with your roof.

Asking these questions shows your contractor you're thoughtful and engaged. It also weeds out contractors who aren't organized or transparent. Trust your instincts: if a contractor seems annoyed by questions or won't provide details, find someone else.

A good roofing contractor will welcome these questions and answer them thoroughly. Taking time now to vet your contractor carefully protects your home and your investment for years to come.
