Questions to Ask Your Roofing Contractor Before You Hire Them

Hiring a roofing contractor is one of the biggest home improvement decisions you'll make—and it's not something you want to get wrong. Asking the right questions upfront helps you avoid costly mistakes, hidden fees, and poor workmanship.

Does Your Contractor Have Proper Licensing and Insurance?

This is non-negotiable. Before anything else, verify that your contractor holds a valid roofing license in your state and carries both general liability and workers' compensation insurance. Request proof of both, and don't accept verbal assurances alone. Ask for the policy numbers and verify coverage directly with the insurance companies if needed.

A licensed contractor has met professional standards and can be held accountable if something goes wrong. Without proper insurance, you could be liable for injuries on your property or damage to neighboring homes.

Can You Provide References and Examples of Past Work?

Ask for at least three recent references from homeowners whose roofs they've installed or repaired in the last two years. Call these references and ask specific questions about the quality of work, whether the project finished on time and on budget, and how the contractor handled problems.

  • Request before-and-after photos of completed projects
  • Ask about roofs similar in size and material to yours
  • Check online reviews on Google, Yelp, and the Better Business Bureau
  • Visit completed projects in your area if possible to see their work firsthand

What Materials Do You Recommend, and Why?

Different roofing materials—asphalt shingles, metal, tile, or slate—have different costs, lifespans, and benefits. A good contractor should explain why they recommend a specific material for your home and climate, not just what's cheapest.

Ask about:

  • The warranty on materials (manufacturer coverage, usually 20-30 years for quality shingles)
  • The warranty on labor (typically 5-10 years)
  • How long the material is expected to last
  • Whether the material matches your home's style and local building codes

Don't just pick the cheapest option. A slightly more expensive, higher-quality material often saves you money in the long run through fewer repairs and longer lifespan.

What's Your Timeline, and What's Included in Your Estimate?

Ask for a detailed, written estimate that breaks down the cost of materials, labor, and any additional work (like removing old shingles or repairing decking). The estimate should also specify the project timeline and when they expect to start and finish.

Red flags include:

  • Vague cost estimates with no itemization
  • Pressure to pay a large deposit upfront (typically 25% is standard)
  • Cash-only payments (legitimate businesses accept checks and credit cards)
  • Unusually low estimates compared to other bids
  • No mention of permits or inspections

Also ask about disposal of old roofing materials and whether the price includes cleanup at the end of each day.

How Will You Keep Me Informed During the Project?

A professional contractor should explain how they'll communicate with you as the work progresses. Will they send daily updates? Can you call with questions? How will they handle unexpected issues like rotted wood or structural damage discovered during installation?

Ask whether they use project management software that gives you real-time visibility into the work. Many reputable contractors now use tools like Roofing OS, which helps keep homeowners informed every step of the way with updates, photos, and timelines right on your phone.

Final Thoughts

Taking time to ask these questions now protects you from headaches later. Get at least three estimates, compare details carefully, and choose a contractor who listens to your concerns and answers your questions thoroughly. A quality roof is a long-term investment in your home's safety and value—it deserves careful attention from a skilled professional.
