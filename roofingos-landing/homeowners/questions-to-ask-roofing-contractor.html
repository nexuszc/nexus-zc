10 Essential Questions to Ask Your Roofing Contractor

Choosing the right roofing contractor is one of the biggest decisions you'll make as a homeowner. Asking the right questions upfront can save you thousands of dollars and prevent headaches down the road.

Ask About Experience and Credentials

Before hiring anyone to work on your roof, you need to know they're qualified. A reputable contractor should have years of experience and proper licensing in your state.

  • How long have you been in the roofing business?
  • Are you licensed, bonded, and insured?
  • Can you provide references from recent projects?
  • Do you specialize in my roof type (asphalt shingles, metal, flat roof, etc.)?
  • Are your workers employees or subcontractors?

Don't skip the reference check. Call at least three previous customers and ask about their experience, whether the work was completed on time, and if they were satisfied with the results.

Understand the Scope and Materials

Your contractor should clearly explain what work will be done and what materials will be used. Different materials have different lifespans and price points.

  • What specific work will be performed (inspection, repair, replacement)?
  • What roofing materials do you recommend, and why?
  • How long will different materials last?
  • What warranty comes with the materials and labor?
  • Will you remove the old roof, or layer over it?

Make sure you get these details in writing. A vague estimate is a red flag—you deserve a detailed breakdown of everything included in the project.

Get Clear Pricing and Payment Terms

Never choose a contractor based on price alone, but you absolutely should understand their pricing structure and payment schedule.

  • Can you provide a detailed written estimate?
  • Is the estimate binding, or could costs change?
  • What's your payment schedule (deposit, progress payments, final payment)?
  • Do you offer financing options?
  • Will you handle my insurance claim if applicable?
  • What happens if unexpected damage is found during the project?

Be suspicious of contractors who demand large upfront payments or want all cash. Standard practice is a reasonable deposit (usually 10-25%), with the balance due upon completion.

Ask About Timeline and Communication

A roofing project can disrupt your daily life, so knowing the timeline and how you'll stay informed is essential.

  • How long will the project take?
  • What's your current workload? When can you start?
  • How will you protect my property during the work?
  • Who is my main point of contact?
  • How will you keep me updated on progress?
  • What will happen if weather delays the project?

Good contractors use modern project management tools to keep homeowners informed every step of the way. Your contractor should be able to tell you exactly how communication will work throughout the project.

Clarify Post-Project Responsibilities

Your relationship with the contractor shouldn't end the moment the work is done. Ask about cleanup, warranties, and follow-up support.

  • Will you clean up debris and haul away old materials?
  • What warranty covers defects in labor?
  • How long is the manufacturer's warranty?
  • What should I do if I notice a problem after the project?
  • Will you perform a final inspection with me?

A professional contractor should stand behind their work and be willing to address any issues that arise shortly after completion.

Final Thought

Taking time to ask these questions now will give you confidence in your choice and protect your investment.
