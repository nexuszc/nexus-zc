Questions to Ask Your Roofing Contractor Before Hiring

Choosing the right roofing contractor is one of the most important decisions you'll make for your home. Asking the right questions upfront protects your investment, sets clear expectations, and helps you avoid costly mistakes down the road.

Experience and Credentials

Before you sign anything, you need to know who you're trusting with your roof. Ask your contractor how long they've been in business and how many roofs they've completed. Don't just take their word for it—request references from recent projects similar to yours.

  • Are you licensed and insured in this state?
  • Can you provide proof of workers' compensation insurance?
  • What certifications do you hold (e.g., manufacturer certifications)?
  • Have you ever had complaints filed against you with the Better Business Bureau?
  • Can you provide at least three references from homeowners in my area?

Scope of Work and Materials

Roofing isn't one-size-fits-all. Your contractor should be able to explain exactly what work they'll do and what materials they'll use. This prevents surprises when the bill arrives.

  • What type of roofing material do you recommend for my home and why?
  • Will you remove old roofing or install over the existing roof?
  • What's the warranty on materials versus workmanship?
  • Are there upgrades available (ventilation, insulation, impact-resistant shingles)?
  • What's included in your quote—underlayment, flashing, gutters, cleanup?

Timeline and Project Management

You need to know when the work starts, how long it will take, and how your contractor will keep you informed. A professional contractor should have a clear timeline and a system for communication.

  • When can you start, and how long will the project take?
  • What's your process if weather delays the work?
  • How will you protect my landscaping and property during the project?
  • Will there be a site supervisor on-site daily?
  • How will you communicate with me about progress and any issues that arise?

Cost and Payment Terms

Price matters, but it shouldn't be your only factor. A suspiciously low bid often means corners will be cut. Get detailed written estimates from at least three contractors and understand what each price includes.

  • Can you provide a detailed written estimate that breaks down labor and materials?
  • What's your payment schedule (deposit, progress payments, final payment)?
  • Do you offer financing options?
  • Will you handle insurance claims if this is storm damage?
  • What's your policy if the final cost exceeds the estimate?

Warranty and Post-Project Support

A roof should last 15-30+ years depending on the material. Your contractor should stand behind their work and be available if problems arise after completion.

  • What's your workmanship warranty, and how long does it last?
  • Will you provide a written warranty document?
  • How do I file a warranty claim if needed?
  • Do you offer routine maintenance or inspection services after installation?
  • Who's responsible for repairs if issues appear within the first year?

Taking time to ask these questions shows you're a thoughtful homeowner, and it helps your contractor understand that you're serious and informed. The best contractors welcome these questions—it's part of building trust and setting up a successful project.
