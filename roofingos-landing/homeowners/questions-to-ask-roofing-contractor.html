Questions to Ask Your Roofing Contractor Before Hiring

Hiring a roofing contractor is one of the biggest home improvement decisions you'll make, yet many homeowners skip the important conversation step. Asking the right questions upfront protects your investment, clarifies expectations, and helps you avoid costly surprises down the road.

Is Your Contractor Licensed and Insured?

This is non-negotiable. Before anything else, verify that your contractor holds a valid roofing license in your state and carries both liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage. Ask to see proof of both—don't just take their word for it.

  • Request their license number and independently verify it with your state's licensing board
  • Ask for a Certificate of Insurance showing current coverage dates
  • Confirm their insurance will cover any injuries or property damage on your property

Uninsured contractors might offer lower prices, but you could be liable if someone gets hurt on your property during the job.

What Materials Do You Recommend and Why?

Roofing materials vary widely in cost, lifespan, and performance. Your contractor should explain why they're recommending specific shingles, metal, tile, or other materials based on your climate, roof pitch, and budget.

  • Ask about durability—how long will the material last?
  • What's the warranty coverage (material vs. labor)?
  • How does the material perform in your local weather conditions?
  • Are there maintenance requirements you need to know about?

A good contractor will discuss options at different price points and help you understand the long-term value, not just the upfront cost.

What's the Complete Cost and Timeline?

Get a detailed, written estimate that breaks down labor, materials, permits, and any additional fees. Don't accept vague pricing—ask specifically what's included and what isn't.

  • How long will the project take from start to finish?
  • Will work happen every day, or are there breaks?
  • What happens if weather delays the project?
  • What's your payment schedule (deposit, progress payments, final payment)?
  • Will you pull necessary permits, or is that my responsibility?

Be cautious of contractors who want large upfront deposits or cash-only payments. A typical deposit is 25-50%, with the balance due upon completion.

Can You Provide References and Examples of Past Work?

Ask for at least three recent references from customers in your area. Call them and ask about their experience—did the work get done on time, was the crew respectful, and did the roof hold up well? If possible, ask to see photos of completed projects or visit one if the homeowner allows it.

  • How long has the company been in business?
  • Are they a solo operator or a larger company?
  • Do they have online reviews you can check?
  • Will they warranty their work in writing?

How Will You Keep Me Informed During the Project?

Communication matters. Ask how often you'll get updates and how you'll communicate—phone calls, text messages, emails, or an online portal. Good contractors keep homeowners in the loop about progress, unexpected issues, and timeline changes.

  • Will there be a designated point of contact?
  • How will the crew minimize disruption to your property?
  • What happens if problems are discovered during the work?

Many professional contractors now use job management software that gives homeowners real-time visibility into their project. Take your time with these conversations. The right contractor will answer your questions thoroughly and patiently. Trust your instincts, and don't hire someone just because they're the cheapest option.
