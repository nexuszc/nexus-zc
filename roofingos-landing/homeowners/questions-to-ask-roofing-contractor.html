Essential Questions to Ask Your Roofing Contractor Before You Hire

Hiring a roofing contractor is one of the biggest home improvement decisions you'll make, and it's natural to feel uncertain about what to ask. Getting clear answers upfront protects your investment, sets realistic expectations, and helps you avoid costly surprises down the road.

What's Your Experience and Licensing?

This is where you start. You need a contractor who knows what they're doing and operates legally in your state.

  • How many years have you been in the roofing business?
  • Are you licensed, bonded, and insured in our state?
  • Can you provide references from recent customers?
  • Do you specialize in my type of roof (asphalt shingles, metal, tile, etc.)?
  • Are you certified by any roofing manufacturers?

Don't skip the reference calls. A quick conversation with past clients tells you more about reliability and quality than any marketing material.

What's the Total Cost and What Does It Include?

Money conversations feel awkward, but they're essential. You need a detailed, written estimate before any work begins.

  • What's the total project cost, broken down by labor and materials?
  • Are there potential extra charges (like structural repairs or disposal fees)?
  • What payment schedule do you expect?
  • Do you offer a warranty on labor and materials?
  • Will you handle permit fees, or is that my responsibility?

Compare estimates from at least three contractors. If one quote is significantly cheaper, ask why—sometimes it means corners will be cut.

How Long Will the Project Take?

Roof work affects your daily life and home security. You deserve a realistic timeline.

  • When can you start, and what's your estimated completion date?
  • How many crew members will be working on my roof?
  • What happens if weather delays the project?
  • Will you work on weekends or evenings if the job takes longer?
  • How will weather events like rain impact the schedule?

Ask specifically about their rain policy. A professional contractor won't continue work during heavy rain—it compromises quality and safety.

What About Cleanup and Safety?

A roofing project is messy. You want assurance that your home and yard will be treated respectfully and safely.

  • How will you protect my landscaping and property during work?
  • What's your daily cleanup process?
  • Who's responsible for disposing of old roofing materials?
  • Do you have liability insurance in case of damage or injury?
  • What safety precautions do you take to protect workers and my family?

Good contractors have detailed answers to these questions. If they seem vague, that's a red flag.

What Happens After the Project Is Complete?

Your relationship with your contractor shouldn't end the moment they leave. Ask about ongoing support and warranties.

  • What warranty do you provide on the roof itself?
  • How long is the labor warranty?
  • Who handles warranty claims if something goes wrong?
  • Will you conduct a final walkthrough inspection with me?
  • Can you recommend a maintenance schedule?

A strong warranty gives you peace of mind. Most reputable contractors offer at least a 5-10 year labor warranty on top of manufacturer warranties on materials.
