Essential Questions to Ask Your Roofing Contractor Before Hiring

Choosing the right roofing contractor is one of the biggest decisions you'll make as a homeowner. Asking the right questions upfront helps you avoid costly mistakes, unexpected delays, and poor workmanship.

Ask About Licensing, Insurance, and References

Before anything else, verify that your contractor is properly credentialed. This protects you legally and financially.

  • Are you licensed to work in our state? — Every state has different requirements. Ask to see their current license and verify it's in good standing.
  • Do you carry liability and workers' compensation insurance? — This covers you if someone gets injured on your property or if damage occurs. Ask for proof of active coverage.
  • Can you provide references from recent projects? — Request at least three homeowner references from work completed in the last two years. Call them and ask about quality, timeline, and cleanup.
  • Are you bonded? — A surety bond protects you if the contractor fails to complete the job or doesn't pay suppliers.

Understand the Scope of Work and Timeline

A clear project scope prevents misunderstandings and surprise costs. Get specifics about what's included and how long everything will take.

  • What exactly will be replaced or repaired? — Ask them to walk you through the work. Will they remove old shingles or install over them? Will they replace the underlayment or flashing?
  • How long will the project take? — Most roof replacements take 1–3 days, but weather and complexity matter. Get a realistic timeline in writing.
  • What happens if weather delays us? — Ask how they handle rain, wind, or extreme temperatures, and whether they'll reschedule for free.
  • Will you handle permits and inspections? — Some projects require building permits. Clarify who handles this and whether the cost is included.

Get Details About Materials and Warranty

The materials your contractor uses directly affect how long your roof lasts. Understanding warranty options protects your investment for years to come.

  • What roofing materials do you recommend and why? — Ask about options (asphalt shingles, metal, tile, etc.), their durability, and cost differences.
  • What warranty do you offer on labor? — Most reputable contractors offer 5–10 year labor warranties. Get the terms in writing.
  • What's the manufacturer's warranty on materials? — High-quality shingles often come with 20–30 year warranties. Confirm you'll receive the warranty paperwork.
  • Do you offer any guarantees against leaks? — Some contractors warranty their work against leaks for a specific period.

Clarify Pricing and Payment Terms

A detailed estimate prevents bill shock. Understand exactly what you're paying for and when.

  • Can you provide a detailed written estimate? — It should break down materials, labor, permits, and cleanup separately. Compare estimates from 2–3 contractors.
  • What's your payment schedule? — Avoid contractors who ask for full payment upfront. Most require a deposit (typically 25–50%), then final payment upon completion.
  • Do you charge extra for debris removal and cleanup? — Confirm whether hauling away old materials is included or costs extra.
  • Are there any hidden fees I should know about? — Ask about charges for underlayment, flashing, gutters, or roof penetrations.

Ask About Communication and Project Management

Good contractors keep you informed every step of the way. Clear communication prevents frustration and ensures you know what to expect.

  • How will you keep me updated during the project? — Ask how often they'll communicate and who your main contact is.
  • What's your process if problems are discovered during work? — Roof issues sometimes emerge once work begins. Understand how they'll handle unexpected costs and decisions.
  • Can I inspect the work before you leave? — Request a final walkthrough to verify quality before paying the balance.
