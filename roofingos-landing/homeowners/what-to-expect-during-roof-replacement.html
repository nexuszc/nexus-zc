What to Expect During Roof Replacement: A Homeowner's Guide

Roof replacement can feel overwhelming if you've never been through it before. Understanding what happens from start to finish will help you prepare your home, set realistic expectations, and know when something isn't going as planned.

Before the Work Begins

Your contractor will schedule an inspection and walkthrough before any work starts. During this visit, they'll assess your roof's current condition, check for structural damage, and discuss material options with you. This is also when they'll provide a detailed estimate, timeline, and answer your questions.

A few days before the work begins, confirm the exact start date and ask your contractor about:

  • What time they'll arrive and when they'll finish each day
  • Where they'll park their trucks and store materials
  • Whether you need to move your cars or clear your driveway
  • How long the entire project will take (typically 1–3 days)

The First Day: Tear-Off and Prep

Expect noise, dust, and activity. Your roofing crew will remove your old shingles, underlayment, and any damaged wood. You'll hear hammering, see nails and debris fall into your gutters, and notice loose granules in your yard—this is all normal.

Once the old roof is stripped away, the team inspects the underlying structure. If they find rotted or damaged decking, they'll let you know before replacing it. This discovery sometimes adds a day or two to the project, but it's essential for a durable roof.

On day one, protective tarps may be placed over your landscaping and AC units. Your contractor should also cover any exposed areas to protect your home from weather.

Days Two and Beyond: Installation

New underlayment goes down first, followed by felt or synthetic barriers for weather protection. Then comes the new roofing material—whether asphalt shingles, metal panels, or another product you selected.

Installation is methodical. Crews work in sections, securing materials with nails or fasteners and applying sealant where needed. The roof is built in layers, and each layer must be done correctly before moving to the next.

Key things to know during installation:

  • Noise will continue but is usually heaviest in early morning hours
  • Your roof will look incomplete partway through—this is expected
  • Workers may need to access your property for water, trash disposal, or to park equipment
  • Work may pause for rain or high winds (safety first)

Final Inspection and Cleanup

Once all roofing materials are installed, your contractor performs a thorough inspection. They check for proper alignment, secure fastening, and correct flashing around chimneys, vents, and skylights. They'll also verify that all valleys and edges are sealed.

Next comes cleanup. The crew will sweep your roof, remove protective coverings, and haul away old materials. They should rake your yard to collect fallen nails and debris, though some may still turn up—metal detectors are often worth the investment.

Before signing off, walk around your home with your contractor. Ask them to point out:

  • New ventilation or flashing details
  • Warranty information and what it covers
  • How to care for your new roof (cleaning, maintenance schedule)
  • Contact info if issues arise after they leave

After Replacement: What's Normal

New shingles sometimes have a strong smell—this fades within days. You might notice loose granules on your roof or gutters for the first month; this decreases over time. Some creaking or settling is normal as materials expand and contract with temperature changes.

If leaks, loose shingles, or other problems appear within your warranty period, contact your contractor immediately.
