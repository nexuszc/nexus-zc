What to Expect During Roof Replacement: A Homeowner's Guide

Replacing your roof is one of the biggest home improvement projects you'll undertake, and it's natural to feel uncertain about what the process actually looks like. Understanding what happens from day one through completion will help you prepare your home, family, and schedule for a smooth project.

Pre-Replacement Preparation and Planning

Before your contractor arrives with their crew, you'll have an inspection and estimate visit. They'll assess your current roof's condition, measure the square footage, and discuss material options with you. Once you've signed the contract, your contractor will schedule the work and provide you with a start date and estimated timeline—usually three to five days for a standard residential roof.

A few weeks before work begins, you should prepare your home. Move vehicles out of the driveway so crews have space to position equipment and dumpsters. Trim back tree branches hanging over your roof, and consider moving outdoor items like patio furniture, grills, and potted plants away from the work area. Inside, let your family know there will be noise and activity on the roof, and schedule any important meetings or quiet time away from home if needed.

Day One: Removal and Preparation

The first day is typically the loudest and most active. Your crew will arrive early with a dumpster and begin removing your old roofing materials. You'll hear tearing, pounding, and constant activity as they strip away shingles, nails, and the old underlayment down to the wooden roof deck.

Once the old materials are cleared, the crew will inspect the underlying structure for damage. If rotted wood or structural issues are discovered, your contractor will contact you to discuss additional costs before proceeding. This is why the first day often runs long—they need to assess what's actually under your old roof.

  • Expect noise from approximately 7 a.m. to 5 p.m.
  • Don't be alarmed by the amount of debris—it's normal for an old roof to take up significant space
  • Keep pets indoors to prevent them from getting stressed or injured
  • Keep children away from the work area and falling debris zones

Days Two Through Four: Installation and Progress

With the old roof removed, installation begins. Your crew will install new underlayment (a protective barrier against moisture), then begin laying down new shingles or roofing materials in rows, working from the eaves toward the roof peak. Each row is carefully nailed or adhered and overlapped to ensure water runs down and off your roof properly.

On these middle days, you'll see visible progress as new material covers the bare wood. The crew will also install flashing around chimneys, vents, and skylights—these details are crucial for preventing leaks. While activity continues, it's typically quieter than day one since most work involves precise placement rather than demolition.

  • Progress should be visible daily
  • Weather delays are common—heavy rain or wind may pause work temporarily
  • Your contractor should update you on timeline adjustments promptly

Final Day: Cleanup, Ridges, and Inspection

The last day wraps up installation with ridge cap shingles at the roof's peak, then moves into cleanup mode. Your crew will walk the property with a magnet to collect nails, rake debris into the dumpster, and remove protection boards from landscaping. A quality contractor will leave your property in better condition than they found it.

Before the crew leaves, your contractor will do a final walkthrough inspection with you. This is your opportunity to ask questions and confirm everything meets expectations. You should receive detailed documentation of all work completed and warranty information for your new roof.

After Replacement: Warranty and Maintenance

Most new roofs come with a manufacturer's warranty (typically 20–50 years) and a contractor workmanship warranty (5–10 years). Keep these documents safe and follow any recommended maintenance, like gutter cleaning, to preserve your warranty.
