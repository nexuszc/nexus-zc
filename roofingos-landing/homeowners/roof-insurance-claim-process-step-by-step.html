Roof Insurance Claim Process: A Step-by-Step Guide for Homeowners

Dealing with roof damage is stressful enough without wondering how to navigate your insurance claim. We'll walk you through the entire process so you know exactly what to expect and how to protect your interests every step of the way.

Step 1: Document the Damage Immediately

The first thing you should do after discovering roof damage is take photos and videos. Use your smartphone to capture wide shots of the affected areas as well as close-ups that clearly show the damage. Don't enter your attic or climb onto your roof if it's unsafe—exterior photos are usually sufficient. Document the date and time, and write down what caused the damage (storm, wind, hail, fallen tree, etc.).

If there's immediate danger, such as a large hole or risk of water intrusion, take temporary measures like placing a tarp over the damaged area. Keep receipts for any emergency repairs, as these may be reimbursable.

Step 2: File Your Claim With Your Insurance Company

Contact your homeowner's insurance company as soon as possible. Most policies have time limits for filing claims, so don't delay. When you call, have the following information ready:

  • Your policy number
  • The date the damage occurred
  • A description of what happened
  • Photos and videos of the damage
  • Any temporary repairs you've made

Your insurance company will assign a claim number and schedule an adjuster to inspect the damage. Ask how long the inspection typically takes and whether you need to be present (usually you should be).

Step 3: Prepare for the Insurance Adjuster's Inspection

The adjuster will visit your home to assess the damage and determine the claim value. Before they arrive, make sure you:

  • Gather all photos and documentation you've collected
  • Have your policy information available
  • Prepare a list of questions about coverage and deductibles
  • Ensure safe access to your roof or attic (if applicable)
  • Have a family member or trusted friend present as a witness

During the inspection, stay present and point out all damage areas. Take notes on what the adjuster says and photographs they take. Ask for a copy of their inspection report once it's complete.

Step 4: Review the Claim Decision and Estimate

After the inspection, your insurance company will send you a claim decision letter and estimate for repairs. Carefully review this document against your own documentation. The estimate should cover all damage you observed. If you disagree with the estimate or feel areas were missed, you have the right to request a re-inspection or hire an independent adjuster to review the claim.

Pay attention to your deductible—this is the amount you'll pay out-of-pocket before insurance coverage kicks in. Also note any coverage limits or exclusions that might affect your claim.

Step 5: Obtain Repair Quotes and Schedule Work

Once your claim is approved, get quotes from 2-3 licensed roofing contractors. Your insurance estimate should be competitive with market rates. Share the insurance estimate with your contractor so they understand what's covered.

When your contractor begins work, your insurance company may require a final inspection before issuing payment. Keep all receipts and invoices, and maintain communication with both your insurer and contractor throughout the process.

A good roofing contractor will handle much of this communication for you, and many use software like Roofing OS to keep you informed about claim status, timeline, and next steps in real-time. This transparency helps ensure the process moves smoothly and you're never left wondering what's happening.

The roof insurance claim process doesn't have to be confusing. By staying organized, documenting everything, and asking questions when needed, you'll protect yourself and get your roof repaired quickly.
