Roof Insurance Claim Process: Step-by-Step Guide for Homeowners

If a storm has damaged your roof or you've discovered an insurance-covered issue, filing a claim can feel overwhelming. This guide walks you through each step of the roof insurance claim process so you know exactly what to expect and how to protect your interests.

Step 1: Document the Damage Thoroughly

Before contacting your insurance company, document everything. Take clear photos and videos of all visible roof damage, both from the ground and (safely) from accessible angles. Include wide shots showing the overall damage pattern and close-ups of specific problem areas like missing shingles, leaks, or punctures.

  • Photograph damage from multiple angles and in good lighting
  • Include the date and timestamp on your photos if possible
  • Keep a written list of all damaged areas you notice
  • Don't throw away damaged materials—keep samples if safe to do so
  • Document the date the damage occurred or was discovered

Step 2: Contact Your Insurance Company Promptly

Call your insurance agent or the claims department as soon as possible after discovering damage. Most policies have time limits for filing claims, so don't delay. When you call, have your policy number ready and be prepared to describe the damage and what caused it. The insurance company will create a claim file and assign an adjuster to your case.

Keep detailed notes of everyone you speak with, including names, dates, and what was discussed. Request a claim number and confirmation that your claim has been filed.

Step 3: Schedule an Inspection with the Insurance Adjuster

Your insurance adjuster will contact you to schedule a time to inspect the damage. This is a critical step—the adjuster's assessment will determine what your insurance covers and how much they'll pay. During the inspection, they'll examine your roof's condition, take their own photos, and review your policy coverage.

  • Be present during the inspection to answer questions
  • Point out all areas of damage you've documented
  • Ask the adjuster to explain their findings in detail
  • Request a copy of the inspection report
  • Consider having your own roofer present for a second opinion

Step 4: Review the Insurance Company's Estimate

After the inspection, your insurance company will provide an estimate for repairs. This estimate may or may not match the actual cost of roof replacement or repair. Review it carefully against quotes from licensed roofers in your area. If you believe the estimate is too low, you have options.

Some homeowners get independent estimates from contractors to compare. If there's a significant difference, you can file a dispute or request an appraisal (outlined in your policy). Keep all contractor quotes organized and clearly labeled with dates.

Step 5: Select a Contractor and Complete Repairs

Once you've resolved any disputes about the estimate, choose a licensed, insured roofing contractor. Get at least two or three quotes before deciding. A reputable contractor will understand the insurance claim process and can help you navigate any remaining issues. They'll coordinate with your insurance company regarding payment and timelines.

Your contractor can also help file any supplemental claims if additional damage is discovered during the repair process—this is common with roof work.

Step 6: Handle Payment and Final Inspection

Insurance companies typically issue payment in two parts: an initial payment after the adjuster's estimate, and a final payment after the work is completed and inspected. Your contractor will guide you through this process. Some insurers pay the contractor directly; others send checks to you. Confirm the payment arrangement before work begins.

After repairs are complete, the insurance company may send another adjuster to verify the work was done properly. Keep all receipts and documentation from your contractor for your records.

Throughout this process, clear communication between you, your insurance company, and your contractor is essential.
