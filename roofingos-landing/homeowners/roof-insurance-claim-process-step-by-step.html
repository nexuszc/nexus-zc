Roof Insurance Claim Process: A Step-by-Step Guide for Homeowners

Discovering roof damage is stressful, especially when you're unsure how to navigate your insurance claim. This guide walks you through each stage of the process so you can get your roof repaired without confusion or delays.

Step 1: Document the Damage Immediately

The moment you notice roof damage—whether from a storm, falling tree, or wear and tear—take action quickly. Document everything with clear photos and videos from multiple angles. Photograph both the damage itself and the surrounding area to show context. Write down the date, time, and weather conditions when the damage occurred. This evidence is crucial for your claim.

  • Use your phone camera or a smartphone—quality doesn't need to be professional
  • Take photos from ground level and, if safe, from nearby higher ground
  • Keep detailed notes about what happened and when
  • Don't make temporary repairs yet; document the damage in its original state first

Step 2: Review Your Insurance Policy and File a Claim

Before contacting your insurance company, review your homeowners policy to understand your coverage limits, deductible, and any exclusions. Most policies cover sudden, accidental damage like storm damage, but exclude wear and tear or neglect. Once you've reviewed your policy, contact your insurance agent or company's claims department. Be prepared to provide your policy number, the date of loss, and a description of the damage.

File your claim as soon as possible. Insurance companies typically have time limits for claim submission, and the sooner you file, the sooner the process moves forward.

Step 3: Schedule the Insurance Adjuster's Inspection

After filing, an insurance adjuster will contact you to schedule an inspection of your roof. The adjuster is responsible for assessing the damage, determining whether it's covered by your policy, and estimating repair costs. During the inspection, be present and point out all damage you've noticed. Provide the adjuster with your documentation, photos, and any professional roof inspections you've obtained.

  • Prepare a list of all visible damage before the adjuster arrives
  • Walk the adjuster through your photos and documented areas
  • Ask questions if you don't understand their findings
  • Request a copy of their inspection report for your records

Step 4: Obtain Repair Estimates

While waiting for the adjuster's report, get repair estimates from licensed roofing contractors. Most contractors offer free inspections and written estimates. Having multiple estimates (typically 2-3) gives you options and helps you understand fair pricing in your area. Share these estimates with your insurance company—they use them as a reference point for their own assessment.

When comparing estimates, ensure each contractor is evaluating the same scope of work. Detailed, itemized estimates are more helpful than vague quotes.

Step 5: Review the Adjuster's Report and Approve the Claim

Once the adjuster completes their inspection, you'll receive a report detailing their findings, the approved coverage amount, and your deductible. Review this carefully and compare it to your contractor's estimates. If the adjuster's assessment seems significantly lower than contractor quotes, you can dispute it and request a re-inspection or hire your own independent adjuster.

After approval, your insurance company will authorize payment. Depending on your policy, you may receive a check made out to both you and the contractor, or directly to you.

Step 6: Complete Repairs and Finalize the Claim

Once funds are approved, schedule your roof repairs with your chosen contractor. During the repair process, your contractor handles communication with the insurance company regarding any additional issues discovered during work. After repairs are complete, the insurance company may request photos of the finished work before releasing final payment.
